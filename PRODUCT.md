# The Fit Lens — thefitlens.com

register: brand

## Product purpose

Marketing site for The Fit Lens, an Android-first body composition app.
The app tracks fat, muscle, and visceral fat (not just scale weight) via
BIA scales and Galaxy Watch, and layers daily AI coaching on top. The
site's only current job is converting visitors into beta signups.

## Users

- Android / Galaxy Watch owners tracking body composition: recomp,
  cutting, building.
- People on GLP-1 medications who need to know whether the weight coming
  off is fat or lean mass. Relevant, never the exclusive framing.
- Technically literate, skeptical of fitness-app hype. They read numbers.

## Brand voice

Confident, terse, declarative. The homepage sets the register:
"Food is primary. Training is the multiplier. Your body is the scoreboard."

- Short sentences. Claims, not adjectives.
- No hype ("Join the revolution!"), no fake urgency ("3 spots left!"),
  no exclamation points, no cheerleading.
- Numbers and mechanisms over lifestyle promises.

## Visual identity

- Monochrome + gold. Near-black surfaces, neutral grays, one warm gold
  accent used sparingly (data, the conversion moment).
- Geist sans throughout, tight tracking on display sizes, strong
  weight/scale contrast.
- Imagery is data made visual (charts, composition), never lifestyle
  stock photography.

## Anti-references

- Qoluna Health territory: clinical blues, sterile dashboard aesthetics,
  "agentic AI" jargon. We are not a clinic.
- Hume Health territory: glossy lifestyle-hardware photography, wellness
  brand tone. We do not sell hardware.
- Generic SaaS landing pages: gradient heroes, icon card grids,
  testimonial carousels.
- Never name competitors in visible copy.

## Strategic principles

(Shared with the app; see the-fit-lens PRODUCT.md.)

1. Show the work, not the cheerleading.
2. One button, one job.
3. Insight before display.
4. Catch the drift, don't report the damage.
5. Earn credibility every screen. Polish is brand.
